1. What is Pollution?

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances or contaminants into the natural environment. It causes adverse changes and can affect air, water, soil, and living beings.



2. Types of Pollution

  • Air Pollution: Harmful gases and particles in the air from vehicles, industries, and burning fuels.
  • Water Pollution: Contamination of rivers, lakes, and oceans due to waste, chemicals, and plastics.
  • Soil Pollution: Loss of soil fertility caused by pesticides, chemicals, and industrial waste.
  • Noise Pollution: Excessive sounds from traffic, factories, and urban areas affecting human health.


3. Effects of Pollution

Pollution has serious impacts such as:

  • Respiratory and heart diseases in humans.
  • Loss of biodiversity and extinction of species.
  • Global warming and climate change.
  • Contamination of food and water sources.
